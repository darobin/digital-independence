Towards European Digital Independence

Building the Euro Stack

A New European Digital Deal. We want the European Union to gain leadership in digital technologies that are crucial for our democracy, our security, and our prosperity.

For this purpose, we must leverage Europe's strengths: openness, diversity, the ability to work together as a network, talent, and creativity. As a first step on that journey we are convening a unique set of leading experts from around the world to share real-world experience and showcase what digital public infrastructure is, how it works, how it maximises benefits for citizens, innovators, public administrations, and how Europe can build it.

  • when: September 24, 2024 from 15:00 to 18:00
  • where: European Parliament
  • convened by: MEPs Axel Voss (EPP), Alexandra Geese (Greens) XXX

In the past mandate, the European Union pioneered key regulation to tackle the dominance of Big Tech gatekeepers and to shape a democratic information ecosystem in the digital realm. Nevertheless, Europe still heavily relies on US tech corporations that stifle competition and innovation, and take possession of the world's creativity. We witness autocratic regimes investing higher shares of GDP in digital technologies than European democracies. Now is the time to create open standards and digital infrastructure that boost the competitiveness of European companies, to seize economic opportunities for cooperation with other parts of the world that have been fostering and investing in digital autonomy (e.g. India, Brazil, Taiwan), and to enhance the resilience of our democracy.

The "European Digital Deal" focuses on three major components:

  • open protocols and open source software operated under democratic governance to liberate the potential and increase the competitiveness of European companies and start-ups;
  • a robust investment programme in a 'European Digital Stack': hardware, bandwidth, infrastructures for data creation, collection, and storage that such protocols will run on;
  • strong and effective competition enforcement and regulation.

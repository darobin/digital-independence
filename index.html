Towards European Digital Independence

Building the Euro Stack

A New European Digital Deal. We want the European Union to gain leadership in digital technologies that are crucial for our democracy, our security and our prosperity.

For this purpose, we should leverage Europe's strengths: openness, diversity, the ability to work together as a network, talent and creativity.

In the past mandate, the European Union has pioneered important regulation to tackle the dominance of Big Tech companies as gatekeepers and to shape a democratic information ecosystem in the digital realm. Nevertheless, we still see Europe heavily relying on US tech corporations that stifle competition and innovation in Europe, and take possession of the world's creativity. At the same time we witness autocratic regimes investing higher shares of GDP in digital technologies than European democracies. Now is the time to create open digital standards and a digital infrastructure that boost the competitiveness of European companies, to seize economic opportunities for cooperation with other areas of the world that have been fostering and investing in digital autonomy (e.g. India, Brazil, Taiwan) and to enhance the resilience of our democracy.

Europe needs to state that it wants to pursue a robust set of open and interoperable digital infrastructure components that empower intelligence at the edges, so as to support European digital services in commerce, cloud, education, healthcare, energy, climate, media, and culture. This will establish a dynamic environment for businesses of all sizes. In turn, European citizens will gain greater agency over their digital lives and the ability to rely on public digital infrastructures governed in an accountable and democratic way.

The "European Digital Deal" focuses on three major components:

  • open protocols, interoperability as well as open source software to unlock the potential and increase the competitiveness of European companies and start-ups;
  • a robust investment programme in a 'European Digital Stack': hardware, bandwidth, infrastructures for data creation, collection, and storage that such protocols will run on;
  • strong and effective competition enforcement and regulation.
